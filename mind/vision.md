# Vision

What rhino-os is becoming. Not identity (that's identity.md) — the product thesis.

## What

A learning plugin for Claude Code that makes products measurably better every session.

You define what your product must do. rhino-os measures whether it does. Every session, the score goes up or the change gets reverted. Over time, the system builds a model of what works for YOUR product — not generic best practices.

## Who

Solo technical founders building products with Claude Code.

Not teams. Not enterprises. Not "developers" in the abstract. One person, shipping alone, who wants their product to get better without hiring. Someone who already talks to Claude in their terminal and wants that conversation to compound.

## How

The loop: **predict → build → measure → update model → repeat.**

- Predict: every action has a hypothesis. Wrong predictions are the training signal.
- Build: /go builds autonomously. Keeps what passes, reverts what doesn't.
- Measure: score = assertion pass rate. One honest number.
- Update: wrong predictions update experiment-learnings.md. The model gets smarter.
- Repeat: each session picks up where the last one left off.

The loop is the product. Everything else — commands, hooks, mind files — exists to make the loop run better.

## What It's Not

- **Not a framework.** No runtime, no dependencies injected into your project. Symlinks and Claude Code extensions.
- **Not a CI tool.** Runs in your terminal, in conversation with you. Not headless.
- **Not a linter.** Health is a gate, not the score. The score is value delivered.
- **Not a deployment platform.** /ship wraps your existing deploy. No lock-in.

## Where It's Going

From "works for one person" to "works for anyone with Claude Code."

Stage now: **early** (one→some). The loop works on HIVE and on rhino-os itself. Next proof: someone who isn't us installs it, runs /plan, builds, and their score improves. No hand-holding.

After that: plugin marketplace distribution. `claude plugin install rhino-os` and it works.

## Success Looks Like

Founder installs. Runs /plan. Builds. Scores improve. Comes back tomorrow. Tells someone.

Not "uses all 9 commands." Not "reads the README." Not "understands the architecture." Just: their product got better, and they know it because the number went up.
