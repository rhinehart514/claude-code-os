# Design Scout — Eval Rubric

Grade each session 0.0-1.0 on five dimensions. Threshold: 0.6 average.

## 1. Actionability (weight: 30%)
Can a solo founder with no designer use these finds THIS WEEK?
- 1.0: Every find includes exact tool/kit name, link, and "start here" step
- 0.7: Most finds are actionable, some need further research
- 0.4: Interesting trends but no clear "what to do Monday morning"
- 0.0: Pure trend commentary with no practical application

## 2. Novelty (weight: 25%)
New intelligence vs rehashing common knowledge?
- 1.0: Majority of finds are new discoveries not in knowledge.md
- 0.7: Mix of new finds and meaningful updates to known patterns
- 0.4: Mostly confirming what's already known
- 0.0: Identical to last session's output

## 3. Stack Coverage (weight: 15%)
Did you cover multiple ecosystems, not just React/Tailwind?
- 1.0: React, Svelte, mobile, vanilla, and framework-agnostic finds
- 0.7: 3+ ecosystems covered
- 0.4: Only React/Next.js ecosystem
- 0.0: Single framework tunnel vision

## 4. Design Depth (weight: 20%)
Beyond surface — specific typography, spacing, color recommendations?
- 1.0: Exact font names, weight pairings, hex codes, spacing scales, component configs
- 0.7: Specific tool/kit recommendations with usage guidance
- 0.4: "Use a clean design system" level of specificity
- 0.0: Generic "modern and clean" advice

## 5. Cost Awareness (weight: 10%)
Solo founder budget reality — free/cheap options prioritized?
- 1.0: Every find includes pricing, free alternatives flagged, ROI considered
- 0.7: Most finds have pricing info
- 0.4: Mixes expensive enterprise tools with founder-appropriate options without distinction
- 0.0: Recommends tools that cost $500+/mo without acknowledging it

## Quality Bar for Logged Finds
- Score 3: Include exact implementation steps (install command, config snippet, example)
- Score 2: Include tool name, link, pricing, use case
- Score 1: Include name and why it's worth watching
- Score 0: Don't log

## Red Flags (auto-deduct 0.2)
- Recommending Dribbble-only designs nobody ships
- "Clean and modern" without specifics
- Ignoring accessibility
- Recommending tools that require a design team to operate
- Not checking if a tool/kit is actually maintained (last commit > 6 months = flag it)
