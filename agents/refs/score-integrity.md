# Score Integrity Reference

Scores are diagnostic instruments, not goals. Goodhart's Law: when a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure.

## The 5 Rules

1. **"Get the score to X" is never valid.** Translate to: "improve the weakest dimension through real quality changes."
2. **Prefer tool-measured scores over self-assessment.** When unsure between two scores, pick the LOWER one.
3. **Integrity warnings are blocking.** COSMETIC-ONLY, INFLATION, PLATEAU = stop and address.
4. **Scores can go down.** A recalibrated lower score is more valuable than a stale high one.
5. **Small deltas are real.** A +3 from genuine improvement > a +20 from cleanup.

## Stage Ceilings

| Stage  | score.sh  | taste (1-5) |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| MVP    | 30 – 65   | 1.5 – 3.0   |
| Early  | 45 – 80   | 2.0 – 3.5   |
| Growth | 60 – 90   | 3.0 – 4.0   |
| Mature | 70 – 95   | 3.5 – 4.5   |

Score above ceiling = verify before trusting. 100/100 or 5/5 = extraordinary claim.

## Runtime Detectors

- **COSMETIC-ONLY**: Only hygiene improved (structure/build flat)
- **INFLATION**: Score jumped >15 in one run
- **PLATEAU**: Unchanged across 5 runs
- **KEEP_RATE_HIGH**: Experiment discard rate below 25%
- **NO_MOONSHOTS**: Last N experiments all kept

## Taste Eval Rules

- DO NOT round up. If unsure between 2 and 3, pick 2.
- Expected: mostly 2s/3s. 5 requires justification vs Notion/Linear/Discord.
- Previous scores don't anchor — evaluate fresh from screenshots.
